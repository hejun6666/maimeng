# AI Shopping Readiness Rules

## Purpose

AI Shopping Readiness means the listing is easy for Amazon shopping assistants and semantic recommendation systems to understand. It does not mean guaranteed ranking, recommendation, indexing, citation, or sales.

## Signals To Make Explicit

Strong AI-readable listing copy should clearly expose:

- product type
- target audience
- use scenarios
- key attributes such as material, size, quantity, compatibility, color, power source, or included items
- concrete benefits tied to those attributes
- setup, care, storage, or portability when relevant
- limitations and facts that require operator verification

Do not bury these facts behind vague claims such as `premium quality`, `perfect choice`, or `must-have`.

## Buyer Questions

Before writing copy, create likely buyer questions. Answer them naturally across the title, bullets, and description instead of adding a visible FAQ unless the category normally supports FAQ-style copy.

Good question types:

- Who is this product for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What is included?
- Where can it be used?
- What size, material, compatibility, or quantity matters?
- How is it different from common alternatives?
- What should the buyer verify before purchase?

## Bullet Roles

Use these roles unless category compliance requires another order:

1. Core buyer promise and audience.
2. Key attributes, build, material, compatibility, or verified safety-related facts.
3. Use experience, setup, maintenance, portability, storage, or daily convenience.
4. Scenarios, audience segments, giftability, or comparison context.
5. Included items, value, care, support, limitations, or differentiation.

Each bullet should contain a benefit-led mini headline and a clear fact or candidate fact that supports the benefit.

## Description Pattern

The description should work as a short buying-guide answer:

1. Identify the product and target buyer.
2. Explain the main use case and problem it solves.
3. Tie attributes to outcomes.
4. Avoid introducing new unverified claims.

## Backend Search Terms

Use backend terms to cover semantic gaps:

- synonyms
- audience terms
- scenario terms
- attribute terms
- common alternate names

Do not repeat exact title phrases excessively. Do not include competitor brands, ASINs, prohibited claims, or AI/GEO promise language.

## Forbidden AI/GEO Claims

Do not use these phrases or close variants in Amazon copy:

- AI recommended
- recommended by Rufus
- Alexa recommended
- Rufus optimized
- GEO guaranteed
- guaranteed ranking
- guaranteed indexing
- guaranteed citation
- guaranteed visibility

Operator notes may say that the copy is designed to improve AI understanding, matching, and citation readiness, but must also state that no recommendation or ranking is guaranteed.

## Common Mistakes

- Making copy robotic by turning bullets into a visible FAQ.
- Stuffing keywords instead of answering buyer intent.
- Adding unverified materials, certifications, age ranges, waterproof claims, medical claims, safety claims, or compatibility claims.
- Letting AI-readability weaken conversion language.
- Mixing Chinese notes into the marketplace-language copy block.

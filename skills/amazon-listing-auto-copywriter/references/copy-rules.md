# Amazon Copy Rules

## Title

- Default target: 150-190 characters, but keep readability above length.
- Include product type, primary differentiator, key size or quantity, major accessory, and use case when known.
- For enhanced candidate copy, use keyword + scenario + result language instead of keyword stuffing alone.
- Do not repeat the same keyword awkwardly.
- Do not include unsupported claims, promotional language, emojis, warranty promises, or competitor brand names.
- For US and CA, write natural English.

## Bullet Points

Use 5 bullets. Each bullet should contain:

1. A benefit-led mini headline ending with a colon.
2. A buyer scene, pain point, or desired outcome.
3. A product fact or candidate feature that supports the promise.
4. Natural keyword inclusion without copying competitor sentences.

Recommended bullet roles:

1. Main buyer promise.
2. Material, build, fit, safety, compatibility, or performance.
3. Size, capacity, installation, portability, storage, or maintenance.
4. Use scenarios and audience.
5. Accessories, package value, care, support, or differentiation.

For enhanced candidate copy, target roughly 45-75 words per bullet. Write like a strong US Amazon listing: concrete household scenes, parent/customer language, and outcome-focused benefits. Avoid official-sounding summaries such as `Suitable for use as...`.

Avoid empty claims such as `premium quality`, `best choice`, `perfect gift`, and `high quality` unless backed by specific facts.

## Product Description

- 1-3 concise paragraphs.
- Explain who the product is for, what problem it solves, and how the verified or candidate features support the use case.
- Keep the voice clear and practical.
- Do not introduce new unverified facts.

## Backend Search Terms

- Use lowercase words or phrases.
- Avoid commas when possible.
- Avoid repeating exact title phrases too much.
- Do not include competitor brands, ASINs, unsupported claims, or prohibited terms.
- Prioritize product type, synonyms, attributes, audience, and use-case terms.

## Enhanced Candidate Copy

Enhanced copy may include competitor-derived high-frequency opportunities, but the English copy must stay clean and directly copyable after verification. Do not put `[待核实]`, Chinese notes, footnotes, or explanatory brackets inside title, bullets, description, or backend search terms.

This is the only default English copy block. Do not output a conservative/confirmed fallback unless the user explicitly asks for it. Deeply blend competitor patterns including repeated keywords, selling angles, use scenarios, and buyer phrasing, but rewrite the language. The goal is strong conversion copy that operators can review, not a conservative product summary.

## Chinese Copy Reference

After the English copy block, provide a Chinese reference version that mirrors the title, bullets, description, and backend search terms. This section is for internal operator reading only.

- Keep the Chinese reference in the same order as the English fields.
- Translate the English copy one-to-one and completely. Do not summarize or turn it into an intent explanation.
- Translate every bullet under the same bullet number.
- Translate the full product description.
- Translate backend search terms as term meanings or phrase-by-phrase Chinese equivalents.
- Do not add new claims in Chinese that are not present in English.
- Do not put verification warnings inside the Chinese copy reference. Put them in the operator review checklist.

Track every unconfirmed claim in the Chinese operator section instead. The checklist item should identify the exact field and bullet number, such as:

- `Enhanced Bullet 3`: confirm whether a mat is included and whether it is waterproof or anti-slip.
- `Enhanced Title`: confirm exact size before using `50 x 50 Inch`.
- `Enhanced Bullet 4`: confirm frame material, suction cups, and connector design.
